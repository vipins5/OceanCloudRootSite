**Goal:** Build expertise credibility, address enterprise concerns

```
Subject: How Scout Aligns with GDPR, HIPAA, and Your Compliance Requirements

Hi [FirstName],

If your organization is governed by GDPR, HIPAA, FedRAMP, or SOC 2 requirements, 
Scout can still be deployed securely.

Here's what compliance looks like in practice:

SCENARIO 1: GDPR-regulated organization
  → Scout data stored in EU region (Germany or Ireland per your requirement)
  → Sensitivity labels prevent Scout from processing PII
  → Audit logs capture all Scout activities (right to access/erasure)
  → DLP rules block Scout from seeing healthcare or financial data
  → Result: Full GDPR compliance with Scout productivity benefits

SCENARIO 2: HIPAA-regulated (healthcare/biotech)
  → Scout excluded from PHI-containing mailboxes via conditional access policies
  → Audit logging enabled for compliance reporting (required for HIPAA breach investigations)
  → Information barriers prevent Scout from crossing department boundaries
  → Custom sensitivity labels mark all medical records as "Scout-restricted"
  → Result: Scout available for administrative work; clinical data remains untouched

SCENARIO 3: FedRAMP (government contractors)
  → Scout deployed in Azure Government Cloud (sovereign data residency)
  → Conditional access enforces network-only (no mobile Scout access)
  → All Scout activity logged and audited per FedRAMP requirements
  → Result: Scout available while maintaining FedRAMP certification

The pattern: Scout respects your existing security controls. 
It doesn't bypass them — it enhances productivity within your governance boundaries.
```
