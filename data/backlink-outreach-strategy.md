## Competitive Backlink Analysis

### Competitors to Monitor
1. **Microsoft Learn** (for Copilot Cowork)
   - Which sites link to official Microsoft guides?
   - Do those sites also link to consultant guides?

2. **Other Consulting Firms** (Deloitte, Accenture, etc.)
   - Where do THEY get backlinks?
   - Can we pitch to those same sites?

3. **Tech Bloggers & Influencers**
   - Who writes about your topics?
   - Who links to THEM?

### Action: Reverse Backlink Analysis
Use SEMrush or Ahrefs:
```
Compare backlinks to:
- microsoft365.community (our ideal resource)
- competitor_consultant.com
- techblog_focused_on_m365.com

Look for: Sites that link to all three
→ These are warm audiences, easy wins for outreach
```

## Special Initiatives

### 1. Webinar Collaboration (High-Value)
- Propose webinar with Microsoft MVP
- Topic: "[Guide Topic] — Real-World Implementation"
- Promote on both channels
- Both guides get mentioned, linked

### 2. Newsletter Features (Medium-Value)
- Reach out to M365 newsletters (15-20 popular ones)
- Ask to feature guide in "Tools & Resources" section
- Provide short summary they can use

### 3. Guide Exchange (Low-Effort)
- Find non-competing guides on similar topics
- Propose mutual linking (no direct exchange, just natural linking)
- Example: Blog about "IT Governance" → links to our "Scout Governance Guide"
