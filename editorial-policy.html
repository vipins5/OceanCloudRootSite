Accountable publishing

Editorial policy & technical review

How OceanCloud publishes technical guidance without unsupported identities, credentials, or claims.

OceanCloud is the accountable publisher of this site. Guides are attributed to the organisation unless a named contributor has a public, verifiable profile. We do not use fictional authors or unsupported certification badges.

Primary-source standard

Technical claims should link to Microsoft Learn, Microsoft Support, PnP PowerShell documentation, product release notes, or another primary source. Secondary commentary is supplementary, not authoritative.

Practical scope

Guides state assumptions, prerequisites, limitations, and production risks. Scripts and configuration steps should be tested in a non-production environment before use.

Original value

We prioritise implementation sequences, decision criteria, failure modes, validation checks, and reusable examples over summaries of vendor announcements.

Updates and dates

A modified date is changed only when the main content, cited platform behaviour, structured data, or material links have changed. Cosmetic edits do not justify a freshness update.

Automation disclosure

Automation may assist maintenance, formatting, and source collection. It is not used as a substitute for attributable evidence, and automated feed pages are excluded from Google Search.

Corrections

Report a technical error or unsupported claim to oceancloudconsults@gmail.com. Material corrections are visible in the site's public change history.

Verification: Readers can inspect the source history behind this website, follow cited documentation, and request evidence relevant to a proposed engagement before relying on a claim.
